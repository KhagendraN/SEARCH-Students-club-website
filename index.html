About SEARCH

Ayurveda students building a research, awareness, and support community in Kirtipur.

Who we are

Welcome to SEARCH Students' Club—Students Enriching Ayurveda Research and Convenient Health, the student-led club of Ayurveda students at Ayurveda Campus, Kirtipur, Kathmandu. We bring together students who want to learn, research, and work for better health awareness and academic growth.

Our focus is on research publication, health awareness, student collaboration, and building a supportive club culture that helps Ayurveda learners grow beyond the classroom.

Non-political and Non-profitable initiative of Students of Ayurveda Campus, helping to enrich in Ayurveda and Convenient Health, established in 2011 A.D.

What we do

How SEARCH turns student energy into awareness, research, and service.

Research-driven

We explore Ayurveda and health-related topics through student research before turning ideas into action and publication.

Student collaboration

We work together across batches and interests so members can learn, contribute, and lead club activities.

Community-minded

We are a non-profit, student-led club centered on service, support, and shared growth.

Service-oriented

A full pipeline: learn → research → share → serve with the wider Ayurveda community.

Our objectives

Core work we optimize for—and supportive programs that grow SEARCH culture.

Primary — core focus

Research
Study Ayurveda-related topics, health issues, and student interests to create meaningful club output.
Publication
Publish academic guides, past year question (PYQs) Collection, and educational resources to support students.
Build
Run student-led activities that strengthen learning, teamwork, and community participation.

Secondary — supportive

  • Events — Health camps, health awareness programs, exams, and many more.
  • Awareness — Programs on health, Ayurveda, and student engagement.
  • Education — Resources for learning, mentorship, and peer support.

Benefits

Why students show up—and what SEARCH gains when members learn together.

For students

  • Real-world, portfolio-building experience through research, events, and club responsibility.
  • Leadership, teamwork, and communication skills through roles and activities.
  • Mentorship and networking with faculty, alumni, and the broader Ayurveda community.
  • Lower barriers for beginners through guided participation and peer support.

For Nepal

  • Stronger student participation in health awareness and service.
  • More research visibility and academic collaboration.
  • A supportive club culture that connects students across batches.
